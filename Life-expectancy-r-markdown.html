Adjusted r square of the created model is 0.8135. Let’s check the histogram and qqplot of the model.

After observing the histogram of the residuals of the Life_model turns out to be leptokurtic, which clearly stands in the way of normality assumption, as we know that histogram of a sample following a normal distribution is always mesokurtic. Hence, the claim for normaity gets discarded. On plotting the qqplot, it can be observed that the quantiles of the residuals of the model(represented by the black bold line in the graph), shows significant deviation from the quantiles of a normal data(represented by the blue straight line in the graph), which once again rejects the normality claim. Histogram and qqplot is enough to draw the conclusions about the normality claim of the data, but still we use Shapiro Wilks test to gain evidence regarding this.

Now chek for influential observation, high leverage points and outliers in the dataset , have to remove them from the dataset.
